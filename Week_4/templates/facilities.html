Divison Of Computer Science and Engineering


Research Center

The Computer Science and Engineering Division at Karunya Institute of Technology and Sciences established a Cisco Center of Excellence in 2007 through an MoU with Cisco Systems, Bangalore. This center offers world-class networking infrastructure and delivers the CCNA (Cisco Certified Network Associate) certification program, which consists of four core modules: Networking Fundamentals, Routing Protocols and Concepts, LAN Switching and Wireless, and Accessing the WAN. The faculty are regularly trained by Cisco Networking Academy, ensuring the curriculum stays industry-relevant. The center is equipped with advanced Cisco hardware (routers, switches, wireless adapters, and security appliances), as well as modern PCs and simulation tools like Packet Tracer. It regularly hosts national and divisional networking competitions (such as Cisco NetRiders) and outreach activities for rural school students. The division has trained numerous students and faculty in CCNA and CCENT certifications, conducted multiple workshops and training sessions, and guided postgraduate research projects with a focus on networking and cloud technologies. Several students have secured placements in leading companies as a result of this certification training.

The Division signed an MoA with the SUSE Academic Program (SUSE Software Solutions, Ireland) on 29th November 2019, strengthening a partnership that began in 2010 with Novell (SUSE as partner). This collaboration led to the launch of the SUSE Certified Administrator (SCA) in Enterprise Linux certification, with ongoing support for both B.Tech and M.Tech programs since 2014. The partnership established a Center of Excellence for Cloud Computing, providing students and faculty with access to SUSE Academy’s online resources, training materials, virtual labs, and free use of SUSE products for education and research. The center also supports placements, local training sessions, and offers continuous technical support and branding. The facility is equipped with advanced servers, networking hardware, desktops, and IoT devices to support hands-on learning and research in cloud computing and open-source technologies

The IBM Big Data Software Centre of Excellence at Karunya Institute of Technology and Sciences was established through an MoU with IBM India Pvt Ltd in August 2015, with the goal of equipping students and faculty with industry-relevant skills in Big Data Analytics using leading IBM software and tools. The center supports research in areas such as biomedical text mining, analytics on surveillance and social media data, drug analysis, predictive analytics for cancer, fraud detection, and traffic analysis.

Key offerings include industry-certified courses, internships, entrepreneurship support, faculty development programs, workshops, webinars, and project-based learning. The lab is equipped with advanced IBM software (Infosphere BigInsight, Datastage, Streams) and hardware (notably the IBM Netezza appliance), and features a four-node Hadoop cluster for analytics projects. Industry mentors provide project and career guidance, while events like hackathons and project competitions foster hands-on experience.

The center has trained faculty and students in Big Data and IBM Cloud Application Development certifications, with significant participation in IBM’s “Call for Code” and other industry contests. The initiative has enabled internships at IBM and provides ongoing certification and curriculum development in collaboration with industry experts.

The Computing Security Research (CSR) Lab at Karunya’s Computer Science and Engineering division is a state-of-the-art research center supporting faculty, research scholars, and students in advanced research on security for networks, cloud computing, big data, IoT, and software-defined networks. The lab organizes workshops and seminars led by industry experts on cyber forensics, ethical hacking, and emerging cybersecurity topics, and maintains an academic partnership with EC Council Academia.

CSR Lab has produced numerous Scopus-indexed research publications and supports both undergraduate and postgraduate projects. Its infrastructure includes 22 desktop computers (Dell and Apple iMac) and a Power Edge R430 server. The lab also plays a key role in curriculum development and provides regular lab support for postgraduate students, with ongoing updates to security-related syllabi.

Laboratories

The Division of Computer Science and Engineering at Karunya Institute of Technology and Sciences signed an MoA with Microsoft Corporation, India on December 18, 2020, to support and endorse its undergraduate and postgraduate programs in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, and Data Science. The partnership provides curriculum integration, Microsoft certification training for students and faculty, access to advanced tools and software, support in learning pathways, and train-the-trainer programs to enhance institutional and educator capabilities.

The Internet of Things (IoT) lab at Karunya Institute of Technology and Sciences offers a robust infrastructure to help students develop innovative, real-world IoT solutions focused on societal needs. Equipped with Raspberry Pi Trainer kits, Arduino, various sensors (heartbeat, air quality, fire, etc.), LoRa WAN devices, and desktop computers, the lab supports hands-on learning in data ingestion, acquisition, machine learning, visualization, and mobility. Lab sessions are led by industry-trained faculty, and specialized faculty development programs have been conducted to keep staff updated on cutting-edge IoT and industrial IoT technologies.

Karunya Institute of Technology and Sciences signed an MoA with EMURGO Academy on November 25, 2019, to provide students with blockchain technology training, certification, and internship opportunities. The "Foundation of Blockchain" course covers core blockchain concepts, cryptocurrency, consensus algorithms, smart contracts, and DApps, preparing students for industry demand across sectors like finance, healthcare, and government. Students receive an EMURGO digital badge upon completion. In the 2019–20 academic year, second- and third-year CSE students completed the course, with two students securing internships at EMURGO, where they gained practical skills in Ethereum blockchain, Solidity programming, Truffle framework, and Web3 with React front-end development. The partnership also supports curriculum development and live project opportunities in blockchain technology..

The Software Product Development Lab in the Division of Computer Science and Engineering was established to foster product-oriented software development using sound engineering principles, encouraging students to form teams and actively build software products. The lab provides a collaborative environment for intensive technology work, group projects, self-study, and demonstrations, supported by modern computing facilities including Apple iMacs and numerous Dell and HP desktops. Over the years, students and faculty have developed a wide range of software products and Android applications, such as language interpreters, penetration testing tools, library management systems, bus route guides, inventory management, and university-related mobile and web applications, showcasing the lab’s strong track record in practical, industry-relevant software development.

Department Library

The Division Library provides the cognitive and intellectual pursuit of the division towards academic and research activities. The library is well equipped with latest collection of books, magazines and reference materials covering all subjects in the area of computer science. The library has around 1957 volumes of text books, reference books and complementary sample books. Books on Artificial Intelligence, Deep Learning, Data Analytics, Advanced computing, Database Management, Programming and Industry campus connect books from Industries such as Infosys, Novell, SUSE, Oracle etc are available for lending and as references. The library has a well-equipped reading facility. Every year the latest books and most recent editions are procured as per the requests and requirement of the faculty and students.

The Division of Computer Science and Engineering Library is located in Room 201 on the 2nd Floor. It houses a collection of 1,957 books and 1,296 unique titles. The library is open on all working days from 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM, and can be contacted at (0422) 2614027. Dr. Esther Daniel serves as the faculty-in-charge of the library.
